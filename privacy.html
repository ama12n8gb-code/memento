Memento — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

The short version

Memento does not collect, store, or share any of your personal data. Everything stays on your phone.

What information the app uses

To show your life in weeks, the app asks for your date of birth, and optionally a parent's age and how often you visit them. This information is saved only in your device's local storage. It is never sent to us or to anyone else. We have no servers, no accounts, and no database.

What we do NOT do

We do not collect names, emails, locations, contacts, or usage analytics. We do not show ads. We do not use tracking or advertising SDKs. We do not sell or share data with third parties, because we never receive any data in the first place.

Third-party services

The app loads its fonts from Google Fonts the first time it runs. This is a standard web request to Google's servers and may expose your IP address to Google, as with any website. After the first load, fonts are cached and the app works fully offline.

Deleting your data

All app data lives on your device. To erase it, clear the app's storage in your phone settings or uninstall the app. Nothing remains anywhere else.

Children

Memento does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever adds features that handle data differently, this page will be updated before those features are released.
